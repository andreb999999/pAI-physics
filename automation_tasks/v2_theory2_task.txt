Formal objectives:

1) SCALING LAW DERIVATION — derive eta*(B) for Muon:
   - For the Muon update W_{t+1} = W_t - eta * polar(grad_B L(W_t)) with batch size B:
     a) If we double B, what happens to the optimal eta? Is there a simple scaling law eta(B)?
     b) Key insight: polar factor has FIXED norm (||polar(G)||_F = sqrt(min(m,n)) exactly, independent of G).
        Therefore increasing B improves the DIRECTION accuracy of the polar factor but does not change its magnitude.
     c) How does the direction accuracy of polar(grad_B) scale with B? At what rate does polar(avg of B gradients) approach polar(full gradient)?
     d) Does Muon follow sqrt(B) scaling (like signSGD) rather than linear scaling (like SGD)? Derive this rigorously.

2) CRITICAL BATCH SIZE:
   - Define B* for Muon: the batch size beyond which polar(avg_grad_B) ≈ polar(full_grad) with high probability.
   - Derive a formula for B*_Muon in terms of problem parameters (matrix dimensions, gradient singular value gap, noise level).
   - Compare B*_Muon with B*_SGD and B*_Adam. Is Muon's critical batch size larger or smaller?
   - What are the practical implications? Does Muon benefit more or less from large batches than Adam?

3) MAJORITY-VOTE INTERPRETATION:
   - The sign/polar function applied to a sum of stochastic gradients can be interpreted as a majority vote on gradient directions.
   - How does vote accuracy scale with B? Derive concentration bounds.
   - Connect to signSGD literature on sign majority votes.

4) EMPIRICAL VALIDATION using NanoGPT data from experiment1:

   - Use the derived formula to predict B*_Muon. Compare with empirically observed saturation point.
   - Submit batch sweep training jobs via SLURM GPU (A100).

5) REGULARIZATION-SCALING BRIDGE (connect to theory1):
