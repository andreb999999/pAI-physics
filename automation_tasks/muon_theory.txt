Theory run for Muon implicit regularization.

Goal:
1) Formalize the conjecture that the Muon optimizer induces implicit regularization: Muon on L is equivalent to GD on L + lambda * R for some regularizer R.
2) Build a complete claim graph with assumptions, definitions, lemmas, and main theorems.
3) Draft rigorous proofs for ALL claims (not just a subset) and run symbolic + numerical verification.
4) Save outputs in the workspace (working_idea.json, math_workspace/claim_graph.json, proofs, checks).

Conjecture:
- Muon update: W_{t+1} = W_t - eta * sign(grad L(W_t))
- We seek R(W) such that Muon dynamics are equivalent (fixed points, trajectory limits, stationary points) to GD on L + lambda * R.
- Key insight: sign(x) is the subgradient of |x|, so sign(grad L) relates to subgradient of the L1 norm of the gradient. This suggests R(W) is connected to the L1 norm of W.
- Start with scalar case, then linear/matrix-factorization setting, then address nonlinear extensions as conjectural.

Required claim graph content (minimum):

Assumptions (at least 4):
- A1: Loss function smoothness (L is C^1 with Lipschitz gradient)
- A2: Bounded gradients (grad L is bounded away from zero or behavior at zero specified)
- A3: Convexity or strong convexity conditions for convergence results
- A4: Finite-dimensional parameter space

Definitions (at least 6):
- D1: Muon discrete update rule: W_{t+1} = W_t - eta * sign(grad L(W_t))
- D2: Muon continuous-time flow: dW/dt = -sign(grad L(W))
- D3: Candidate regularizer R(W) = ||W||_1 (L1 norm)
- D4: Regularized objective: L_reg(W) = L(W) + lambda * ||W||_1
- D5: Fixed-point equivalence: same stationary points
- D6: Trajectory equivalence: matching dynamics under reparameterization
- D7: Coordinate-wise descent interpretation

Lemmas (at least 6):
- L1: sign(x) = subgradient of |x| for all x (connection to L1 subdifferential)
- L2: Muon update decomposes into coordinate-wise independent sign steps
- L3: Fixed points of Muon satisfy |grad_i L(W*)| <= eta for all coordinates i (soft-thresholding condition)
- L4: Stationary points of L + lambda*||W||_1 satisfy the same condition with lambda = eta
- L5: Scale invariance: Muon trajectory on c*L equals Muon trajectory on L for any c > 0
- L6: Muon flow is a gradient flow of L + eta*||W||_1 in the 1-Wasserstein sense (or analogous metric structure)
- L7: Convergence rate: O(1/sqrt(T)) for convex L under Muon dynamics

Theorems (at least 3):
- T1: (Scalar case) For scalar W and convex L, Muon fixed points coincide exactly with minimizers of L + eta*|W|
- T2: (Linear case) For W in R^d and convex L, Muon stationary points are the KKT points of min L(W) s.t. ||W||_1 <= C for appropriate C depending on eta
- T3: (Convergence) Under convexity, Muon converges to a minimizer of L + eta*||W||_1 at rate O(1/sqrt(T))
- T4 (optional): Continuous-time equivalence between Muon flow and gradient flow of the regularized objective
- T5 (optional/conjectural): Extension to matrix case where sign is replaced by matrix sign function and R relates to nuclear norm
