Core conjecture:
- Muon update: W_{t+1} = W_t - eta * polar(grad L(W_t)), where polar(G) = U_G V_G^T from the SVD G = U_G Sigma V_G^T.
- This is steepest descent under the operator (spectral) norm. By norm duality, we conjecture implicit regularization toward minimum nuclear norm solutions.
- We seek R(W) such that Muon dynamics are equivalent (fixed points/trajectory limits/implicit bias) to GD on L + lambda * R.
- Start with linear or matrix-factorization setting; if nonlinear case is too hard, keep it as conjectural extension.

Formal objectives:

1) THE MAIN RESULT — L_1 * A_1 ≈ L_2 * A_2 equivalence (this is the "paper on its own"):
   - Formalize the equivalence: if (L_1, A_1) = (L + lambda * ||W||_*, GD) and (L_2, A_2) = (L, Muon),
     prove or disprove that the stationary points and/or trajectories match.
   - Characterize: under what conditions does this hold exactly vs approximately?
   - How does the effective lambda depend on the learning rate eta, initialization scale, and loss landscape geometry?
   - When does the equivalence break down? What are the sharpest conditions?
   - This should be the central theorem of the claim graph.

2) Regularizer tunability ("play with it"):
   - Consider the interpolated update: W_{t+1} = W_t - eta * (alpha * polar(grad L) + (1-alpha) * grad L)
   - At alpha=1: full Muon (nuclear norm bias). At alpha=0: standard GD (Frobenius norm bias).
   - Characterize the implicit regularizer R(W, alpha) as a continuous function of alpha.
   - Can we continuously tune the strength of nuclear norm regularization by varying alpha?
   - What is the resulting family of regularizers? Is there a clean parametric form?

3) Nonlinear extension:
   - Start with the linear/matrix-regression setting for rigorous proofs.
   - Push to 2-layer networks: W = W_2 * W_1 with Muon applied to each layer independently.
   - Explore effective kernel / NTK perspective for the nonlinear extension.
   - If full nonlinear proofs are too hard, clearly state conjectures with numerical evidence.

4) Generalization connection:
   - When does nuclear norm implicit bias help generalization?
   - Characterize settings where Muon's implicit bias is beneficial vs harmful.
   - Relate to low-rank structure in learned representations and generalization bounds.
